Dress code

We’re getting married so please bring your glad rags! If the weather co-operates then the wedding ceremony and refreshments will be outside (so bear outdoor temperatures in mind) - failing that we have a wet weather plan indoors. The wedding feast and ceilidh will be indoors.

For those of you who are camping, we will provide indoor hanging space for storing dresses and suits. There will also be places for you to get changed / ready and hairdryers to use (or a place to plug in your own).

For the rest of the weekend we encourage typical festival garb - if you’re the kind of person that has ten festival outfits then maybe bring extras to share with those that don’t.

Money

There will be a bar throughout the weekend that takes cash and card. However there will be other activities that will be cash only and there is no cash machine on site (such as mani/pedicures, whisky/gin tastings and highland games). Please bring cash if any of these interest you.

All food and on-site accommodation costs are already included in the payments we calculated from your RSVP.

Arriving

There is designated parking for cars, close to the campsite. The campsite is only a short walk from the car parking and whist cars will be able to drop off gear at the camping area we ask that you move all cars to the parking area once you've unloaded.

Packing list

Here are a few things that you might want to put in your rucksack before you leave home…

  • Midge repellent (it’s Scotland, according to the marines this is the stuff)
  • Sunglasses & suncream (it’s Scotland, but you never know)
  • Blanket(s) for keeping warm around the bonfire
  • Towels (hot showers provided, but bring your own towels)
  • A small torch (you might find this useful if you're camping)
  • Wellies
  • Your best festival outfit (sequins encouraged!)
  • Your wedding outfit and dancing shoes
  • Children (OK, don’t put them in your rucksack)

Camping

Bell tents are booked and allocated - if you are staying in one, signs (and helpful volunteers on-site) will let you know exactly where you are staying.

If you’re in a camper van or a caravan then you’ll be located close to the entrance of the field so that you have access to electricity.

There is a tap near the entrance of the field so you can fill up your water bottles or kettles (that said, we will be providing 24/7 access to hot drinks not far from the campsite).

You are welcome to use gas cookers if you so desire but ask that you refrain from lighting any larger fires and to be aware of the highly flammable (!) hay bales that are scattered around the camping areas. We want a wedding to r emember but not in that way.

If you are in a camper van or caravan please note that there is no Chemical Toilet Disposal Point at Bogbain. Unfortunately the venue has a septic tank that cannot take the chemicals and it’s not viable for us to hire alternatives. That said we’ve spoken to Ardtower Caravan Park, a campsite 3 miles away, who are happy to accept waste for a fee of £10. Given the proximity of the toilets in the main building we recommend you use those for the most part and suggest that you swing by the campsite prior to arriving and/or after leaving Kith & Kin.
