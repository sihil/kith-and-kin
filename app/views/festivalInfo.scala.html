Dress code

Saturday = smart dress, national traditional dress encouraged if you have one.

If good weather we will be getting married outside, so bring a cardigan. If wet, we’ll get married inside. The wedding feast and ceilidh will be indoors.

For those camping, there will be indoor space to hang dresses, suits and the vicar’s robes. There will also be places for you to get ready and plug in your hairdryer!

Friday and Sunday = typical festival get-up (casual, colourful, short shorts, sequins, glitter).

Cash

Bring cash with you! The bar on-site will take cash and card but there are other activities you may want to take part in that need cash. These include:

  • Manicures / pedicures on site on the Friday (£10 - £40 depending what you fancy)
  • Whisky / Gin tasting by a local distillery (£tbc)
  • Highland Games on the Sunday (£tbc).

Packing list

Recommended things to bring in your rucksack:

  • Midge repellent (it’s Scotland, according to the marines this is the stuff)
  • Sunglasses & suncream (it’s Scotland, but you never know)
  • Blanket(s) for keeping warm around the bonfire
  • Towels (hot showers provided, but bring your own towels)
  • A small torch (you might find this useful if you're camping)
  • Wellies (useful in the rain, or just to fit the festival theme)
  • Your best festival outfit (sequins encouraged!)
  • Your wedding outfit and dancing shoes
  • A bottle or two (the venue have agreed that we can drink our own alcohol when the bar is closed)
  • Children (OK, don’t put them in your rucksack)

Recommended not to bring

  • Stiletto heels and farms don’t go very well together. The ceremony will be in a field so unless your get involved activity is providing entertainment for all, we recommend wearing flat shoes (or heel protectors)!

Camping

Bell tents are allocated and they have signs saying who is staying where. When you arrive on site, you’ll be pointed in the right direction.

Campers and caravans are located close to the entrance for access to electricity.

Water: There is a tap near the entrance of the field for drinking water. We will also be providing 24/7 access to hot drinks.

Gas cookers: You are welcome to use gas cookers if you so desire but we ask that you refrain from lighting any larger fires and to be aware of the highly flammable (!) hay bales that are scattered around the camping areas. We want a wedding to remember but rather not in that way.

Toilets and showers: There are 2 blocks toilets and plenty of showers on site. There will be a shower schedule operating on Saturday morning to make sure everyone gets one that needs to.

Charging phones: We'll be setting up a corner where you'll be able to charge phones over the weekend.

Parking

There is designated parking for cars, close to the campsite. The campsite is only a short walk from the car parking and although cars will be able to drop off gear at the camping area please move all cars to the parking area once you've unloaded.

Wedding gifts

We chose not to have a gift list, in favour of a truly participatory wedding where you can give as you like - time, energy, skills or creativity. A few of you have however indicated that you prefer to give a more traditional present, and if that is you, then we would not say no to a contribution to our honey-sun-moon-earth-fund. We’ll be driving the Pacific West Coast of America from San Diego to Seattle, via Madras in Oregon to witness a total solar eclipse on 21 August. All in a Mustang...

We would also very much welcome traditional presents from your home country or something that you think we will treasure for years to come.
